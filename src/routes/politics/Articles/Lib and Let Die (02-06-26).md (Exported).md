# Lib and Let Die

*By Jack Briggs at 10:55PM on  05/07/2026 UTC*  
*Edited by Frankie Wotton*

## Yeah, We Lib'n Out
For eons, people have stomped up the mountain for an ounce of power. Some make a home at where their power stands, content with their lot in life. A few barrel to the top, trying to maximise their power over mankind with every step. , and when they finally get to the top, inevitably abusing their political power, why do some Liberals "Lib out" and attempt to assassinate them?

Everyone knows who Julius Caesar. One of the earliest documented dictators, great politician and strategist. He was assassinated during the ides of March in 44 BCE. The general justification was suspicion he intended to make himself king, on top of other actions circumventing ancient Roman law. So the question is, were the conspirators some of the first guys to ever "Lib out"? 

<CaptionedImage src="https://live.staticflickr.com/3245/3112841424_82042e9ab3_k.jpg" alt="Photo of Caesar (by Ben on Flickr)" capt="Photo of Caesar (by Ben on Flickr)" />

A lot of the conspirators despised dictators, sure. But a chunk of them — like Brutus, who was one of the leaders of the assassination plot — had issues with him not following ancient Roman law or the law not being applied to him, allowing him to do whatever political act he wanted without consequence. Even Cicero, who was not part of the conspiracy but supported it, could be described as an early liberal with his views of the state, laws, and a hate of tyrants. 

This links back to the political climate of today. The repeated failed assassination attempts against President Donald Trump have not come from the fringes of the far left or the far right, but from centrist Liberals. Both Thomas Crooks and Cole Tomas Allen, some of Trumps previous attempted assassins, had Centrist leanings. Crooks had swayed between left and right through his life, with his final position leaning towards centre conservative, while Allen was a known Democrat supporter and considered "blue anon" believer. Even Tyler James Robinson, who is accused of assassinating Charlie Kirk, was a known Centrist Liberal according to his discord messages.

So why are not people from the far-left or far-right orchestrating these assassination attempts? Well, some are, like Lee Harvey Oswald, the man who fatally shot and killed President John F Kennedy in Dallas, Texas on November 22, 1963. But the argument against Oswald was that he assassinated JFK because of his foreign policy against Cuba, not because JFK was considered a dictator. 

Even before Trump 2, Trump was being heavily depicted as a dictator. Through rumours of Project 2025 pre-election, and his obtuse foreign and domestic policy via the Iran war and ICE, most of the attempted assassins have been centrist liberals or centrist leaning. Though both the far-Left and Liberals condemn most political violence, the far-Left through the condemnation of Adventurism and Liberals through avocation of the law, these acts of extreme political violence still seem to keep happening. Though mostly through insanity, I believe there is something deeper to this desire.

## The Breakdown of the Law and Justice
Most Liberals have a seeded faith in the state and the law. To them, the law allows equality and justice, and this is supported and enforced by the state. Though not all laws are perfect to most Liberals, advocating for better laws and reforming outdated or unfair laws is usually their goal for meaningful change. But what if the system is obscenely broken? When if the leaders in charge are twisting and breaking the Liberal state system? Following these questions answers why these guys are "Libbing out".

A lot of left-leaning and right-leaning factions inherently believe the system is already broken in many ways, like flaws in capitalism or like too much being spent on social security. But these are long term grievances that have been accepted, and these sides prefer to try to fix these grievances within the Liberal state system itself with it being easier than toppling and rebuilding a society. Most Liberals believe the same, but a small set believe that, like with replacing parts on an Audi, if its hard to replace and prevents the whole thing from working its got to go. Trump is viewed by many to be the figurehead representing the many broken parts of the US executive branch. Cutting him down will cut down the other parts of the US government that he has seeded his long reaching tendrils into, thus killing the tree at its roots. Though his facist underlings like his second in charge J.D Vance will be sworn in, this is just another head appearing on a proverbial hydra, will the public support this new head? Do these underlings have the chops to woo the MAGA crowd into a docile state? I believe not.

As Trump gets more senile his own chops are degrading as well, not able to move the greater American public with his charisma anymore. Whether his head rolls or not doesn't matter, the Trump dynasty will forever be seen as a stain on America's history. Where he thinks he will be honoured in death like Caesar, his remnants will probably be torn down like Stalins. Either way, its going to be a show, it will be interesting to see from the other side of the world.
